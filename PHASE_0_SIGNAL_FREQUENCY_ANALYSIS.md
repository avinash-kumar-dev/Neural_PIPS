# Signal Frequency vs. Profitability Analysis

## Executive Summary

**More signals per day is NOT always better.** Research shows a clear "sweet spot" where quality and quantity intersect optimally.

**Recommendation for EUR/USD 3:1 TP:SL: 3-6 signals per session (London-NY overlap), hard cap of 5-6 high-conviction trades per day.**

## Why More Signals = Lower Win Rate

### The Quality Degradation Curve
- Trade #1-3: A+ setups (65-80% win rate)
- Trade #4-8: A to A- setups (58-70% win rate)
- Trade #9-15: B+ to B setups (50-60% win rate)
- Trade #16-25: B- to C setups (45-55% win rate)
- Trade 25+: C to D setups (40-50% win rate)

### Transaction Cost Impact
| Trades/Day | Daily Spread Cost | % of Daily Range Consumed |
|------------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| 3 | 3.6-5.4 pips | 4.8-7.2% |
| 5 | 6.0-9.0 pips | 8.0-12.0% |
| 8 | 9.6-14.4 pips | 12.8-19.2% |
| 12 | 14.4-21.6 pips | 19.2-28.8% |
| 20 | 24.0-36.0 pips | 32.0-48.0% |

**Critical**: At 20+ trades/day, you consume 32-48% of EUR/USD's daily range in transaction costs.

### With 3:1 TP:SL Specifically
- If stop is 5 pips, target is 15 pips
- Spread cost per trade: ~1.5 pips average
- As % of profit target: 1.5/15 = 10%
- **At 5 trades/day: 7.5 pips daily cost = 50% of one winning trade's profit**

## Kelly Criterion at Different Frequencies

### Formula
```
f* = p - (q / R)
Where: f* = fraction to risk, p = win rate, q = loss rate, R = reward/risk
```

### Kelly Sizing
| Frequency | Win Rate | Full Kelly | Quarter Kelly |
|-----------|----------|------------|---------------|
| 3-5/day | 65% | 53.3% | 13.3% |
| 8-12/day | 55% | 40% | 10% |
| 15-25/day | 48% | 30.7% | 7.7% |
| 25+/day | 42% | 22.7% | 5.7% |

**Key Insight**: Kelly Criterion mathematically penalizes overtrading. As frequency increases and edge decreases, optimal position size shrinks toward zero.

## Psychological Impact

### Decision Fatigue
- Performance degradation begins at 2-3 hour mark
- Significant impairment after 4+ hours
- Scalpers experience decision fatigue within hours

### Low Frequency (3-8/day)
- Decision fatigue: Manageable
- Revenge trading risk: Low
- Discipline maintenance: Easier

### High Frequency (15+/day)
- Decision fatigue: Severe by hour 2-3
- Revenge trading risk: High
- Discipline maintenance: Progressively harder

## Conclusion

**The sweet spot is not about finding more signals -- it is about finding fewer, better signals and executing them with precision.**

For EUR/USD with 3:1 TP:SL:
- **3-6 signals/day is optimal**
- Concentrated in London-NY overlap
- 0.5% risk per trade maximum
